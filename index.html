Product Strategy & Walkthrough

Ebb: Restructuring corporate calendars around physical & aesthetic stress indicators

1

The User & The Moment

Target Persona: Anya (29)

Anya is a Staff AI Engineer at a high-growth San Francisco scale-up. She is highly ambitious and treats her physical body like a high-performance machine to be bio-hacked and optimized. Her raw pain point is that the sheer cognitive overhead of trying to have it all is the root cause of her biological breakdown—specifically stress-induced hair loss (telogen effluvium) and cortisol-driven adult cystic acne.

A Typical Tuesday Morning without Ebb

  • 7:30 AM: Wakes up physically exhausted (HRV 24ms). Finds a clump of hair on her pillow, checks forehead cystic breakout, and begins to spiral.
  • 8:15 AM: Forces herself onto Peloton to "stay active," but feels lightheaded and stops. Grabs espresso to force alertness, spiking cortisol.
  • 8:45 AM: Calendar has Daily Scrum, Architecture Review (20 people, locked), and Security Spec Drafting.
  • 2:00 PM: Slump hits. She tries to write the security spec on 15% battery but gets distracted. Feels guilty and "unprofessional."
  • 10:00 PM: Apple Watch buzzes to "finish her stand goal," and she vents to ChatGPT.

Where Ebb Enters Anya's Life

At 8:30 AM, after detecting poor wearable recovery, Ebb auto-adjusts her calendar. It leaves heavy multiplayer meetings (scrum standup, review) locked in place as they cannot be rescheduled easily, but slides her alone spec writing block earlier to capture her peak, deletes her intense Peloton session, and inserts a 30-minute recovery buffer right after the architecture review to let her nervous system recover.

2

The Product Itself

Core Value: Passive Sensing & Zero-Log Ledger

Ebb operates on 90% passive sensing and 10% active input (morning voice slider). It recognizes that manual logging is the first thing that dies when a user is exhausted.

Data Type How We Get It Active/Passive
Sleep & HRV Wearables (Oura Ingestion) Passive
Calendar Metadata Google Calendar / Outlook Sync Passive
Telemetry Desktop client keyboard/idle sensor Passive
Subjective Health 3-second morning voice check-in Active

The Key Interaction: Multi-Person Locked Calendar Shifter

Ebb respects corporate scheduling constraints. Unilateral calendar moves only apply to alone working hours (writing specs, admin triage). Multiplayer meetings are locked in place to avoid team conflicts, and Ebb instead injects Active Recovery buffers immediately after these intense sessions.

3

The Hero Metric

Cognitive Alignment Score (CAS)

CAS measures how successfully Anya aligns the complexity of her tasks with her biological capacity, rewarding her for resting when depleted.

CAS = (Time in Focus Peak + Time in Recovery) / Total Working Hours × 100

Why CAS Matters

Traditional tools measure volume (hours worked). CAS measures decision quality in the context of biology. Anya can earn a 100% CAS on a low-energy day if she reschedules alone deep-work blocks and takes recovery walks during slumps.

Unhealthy Gamification & Mitigation

The Risk: Anya might avoid challenging tasks on low-energy days to keep her CAS perfect.

The Fix: Locked team reviews are excluded from CAS penalties, and Ebb caps focus-time scores to reward consistent effort.

4

The Retention Problem

Breaking the 90-Day Churn Cliff

Most wellness apps lose 80% of users by Day 90. Ebb survives because it operates as a structural workflow utility, not a tracking habit.

Workflow Integration vs. Manual Habits

Ebb integrates directly with Google Calendar. When Anya wakes up with a cortisol crash, Ebb automatically reschedules her alone blocks and creates recovery slots. She doesn't need to open Ebb to get value.

5

What We Would Build First (v1 MVP)

12-Week Roadmap Scope

To prove the concept with 1 engineer and 12 weeks, we build the core schedule blocker widget.

  • Keep: A macOS/Windows menu bar app that integrates with Google Calendar and prompts a 3-question morning slider (Sleep quality, Fatigue, HRV mock). It blocks out 90 minutes of "Protected Focus" automatically.
  • Cut: Live Apple Watch API, keystroke activity tracker, team-wide scheduling sync dashboard, and user analytics.

Week 13 Success Metric

Focus Block Adherence

The percentage of Ebb-created calendar blocks that users keep (do not delete) over a 4-week trial.

Target: >70% Adherence
